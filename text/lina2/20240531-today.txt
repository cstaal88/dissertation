--- High-level timeline (20–30 lines; May 31, 2024 → Sep 30, 2025) ---
1) May 31, 2024 — Biden unveils a 3‑phase “Israeli proposal” for a Gaza ceasefire/hostage deal (six‑week truce, IDF pullback from populated areas, phased hostage–prisoner exchanges); frames it as the fastest path to end the war. (White House transcript: https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefing-room/speeches-remarks/2024/05/31/remarks-by-president-biden-on-the-middle-east-2/ ; CNN transcript summary: https://transcripts.cnn.com/show/cnc/date/2024-05-31/segment/08 )
2) May 24–Jun 10, 2024 — ICJ orders Israel to “immediately halt” its offensive in Rafah; then the UN Security Council adopts Resolution 2735 endorsing the U.S. 3‑phase plan. (ICJ order: https://www.icj-cij.org/node/204091 ; UNSC press release: https://press.un.org/en/2024/sc15723.doc.htm ; UN docs page: https://www.securitycouncilreport.org/un-documents/document/s-res-2735.php )
3) June 2024 — Israeli politics shift: Benny Gantz resigns from the war cabinet (Jun 9), after which Netanyahu dissolves the war cabinet (Jun 17), consolidating decision‑making in the security cabinet. (PBS: https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/centrist-israeli-war-cabinet-member-benny-gantz-resigns-over-lack-of-postwar-gaza-plans ; Guardian report on dissolution: https://www.theguardian.com/world/article/2024/jun/17/benjamin-netanyahu-dissolves-israel-war-cabinet )
4) May–July 2024 — U.S. arms posture wobble: Biden pauses one shipment including 2,000‑lb bombs (May), then resumes 500‑lb bombs in July while continuing to hold back 2,000‑lb munitions. (Reuters: https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/us-resume-shipping-500-pound-bombs-israel-us-official-says-2024-07-10/ ; Reuters background on paused 2,000‑lb bombs: https://www.reuters.com/world/us-has-sent-israel-thousands-2000-pound-bombs-since-oct-7-2024-06-28/ )
5) May 22–28, 2024 — European recognition push: Spain, Ireland, and Norway announce and then formally recognize a Palestinian state, amplifying diplomatic pressure on Israel. (Reuters: https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/ireland-recognise-palestinian-state-2024-05-22/ ; https://www.reuters.com/world/spain-ireland-norway-set-recognise-palestinian-statehood-2024-05-28/ )
6) May 2, 2024 → academic year end — U.S. campus protests crest; Biden says “order must prevail” while defending peaceful dissent; House passes the Antisemitism Awareness Act using the IHRA definition (May 1–2), sparking free‑speech debates. (AP/PBS on remarks: https://apnews.com/article/d5f3092671951c3bc2968b8751c93ba6 ; PBS clip: https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/watch-biden-says-order-must-prevail-during-campus-protests-over-war-in-gaza ; Congress.gov H.R.6090: https://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/house-bill/6090 )
7) July 24, 2024 — Netanyahu addresses a joint session of the U.S. Congress; large protests and arrests in Washington and at the DNC in Chicago weeks later underscore activist pressure for a ceasefire/arms embargo. (Transcript: https://www.jns.org/full-text-of-netanyahus-speech-to-joint-session-of-congress/ ; AP on DNC arrests: https://apnews.com/article/democratic-convention-chicago-protests-war-gaza-f064683535bbbe93231d31ecc2dbafc7 ; CBS Chicago tally: https://www.cbsnews.com/chicago/live-updates/chicago-dnc-protest-day-2/ )
8) Summer 2024 — Congress signals backing for major new sales (F‑15s, tank rounds) while reviewing bomb shipments; FMS notifications total >$20B by August. (CRS brief: https://www.congress.gov/crs_external_products/R/PDF/R47828/R47828.30.pdf )
9) May–Nov 2024 — ICC track: Prosecutor seeks arrest warrants (May); on Nov 21, Pre‑Trial Chamber issues warrants for Israel’s Netanyahu and Gallant (and Hamas leaders) and rejects Israel’s jurisdiction challenge—sharply escalating legal jeopardy. (Chatham House explainer: https://www.chathamhouse.org/2024/05/icc-prosecutors-applications-arrest-warrants-explained ; ICC press release: https://www.icc-cpi.int/news/situation-state-palestine-icc-pre-trial-chamber-i-rejects-state-israels-challenges )
10) June 4, 2024 & Jan 9, 2025 — U.S. House passes (2024) and later advances (2025) bills to sanction the ICC over its Israel probe; Senate action lags. (Congress.gov H.R.8282 “Illegitimate Court Counteraction Act”: https://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/house-bill/8282 ; Al Jazeera recap of 2025 House action: https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/1/9/us-house-votes-to-advance-bill-to-sanction-icc-over-israel-arrest-warrants )
11) Jan–Feb 2025 — A U.S.-backed truce takes hold for ~6 weeks with staged hostage releases and large prisoner swaps; intense haggling over a “phase 2” withdrawal stalls, then fighting resumes amid mutual accusations of violations. (Reuters Feb 8/11/18: https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/hamas-set-release-three-israeli-hostages-gaza-ceasefire-holds-2025-02-08/ ; https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/ceasefire-is-only-way-bring-israeli-prisoners-home-hamas-official-says-2025-02-11/ ; https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/israel-begin-negotiations-second-phase-gaza-ceasefire-deal-minister-says-2025-02-18/ ; Reuters Mar 1–2 on standoff/aid: https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/israel-agrees-us-plan-temporary-ceasefire-gaza-pms-office-says-2025-03-01/ )
12) Jan–Feb 2025 — U.S. policy pivot under President Trump: lifts the Biden pause on 2,000‑lb bombs and ships heavy munitions to Israel; later signs an executive order imposing sanctions on the ICC. (Reuters Jan 20 & Feb 16: https://www.reuters.com/world/trump-lift-pause-2000-pound-bomb-supply-israel-walla-news-reports-2025-01-20/ ; https://www.reuters.com/world/israel-receives-shipment-heavy-bombs-cleared-by-trump-2025-02-16/ ; White House EO Feb 6: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/02/imposing-sanctions-on-the-international-criminal-court/ )
13) Spring–Summer 2025 — Northern front: after 2024 escalation with Hezbollah, Israel continues deep strikes in Lebanon; sporadic rocket fire and targeted killings keep risk of wider war high. (Reuters recap: https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/israels-military-set-attack-hezbollah-infrastructure-south-lebanon-2025-09-18/ ; Reuters background on 2024 surge: https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/un-peacekeepers-lebanon-urge-immediate-de-escalation-2024-09-20/ ; Reuters July 15, 2025: https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/israeli-defence-minister-says-strikes-under-way-lebanon-are-clear-message-2025-07-15/ )
14) July 2025 — U.S. public opinion hits a low for support of Israel’s campaign: only 32% approve, 60% disapprove; disapproval is strongest among Democrats and younger voters, feeding protest energy and election‑year organizing. (Gallup: https://news.gallup.com/poll/692948/u.s.-back-israel-military-action-gaza-new-low.aspx ; Brookings analysis: https://www.brookings.edu/articles/support-for-israel-continues-to-deteriorate-especially-among-democrats-and-young-people/ )
15) Activism 2024–2025 — Progressive, faith‑based, and youth coalitions intensify arms‑embargo and divestment campaigns; large‑scale protests surround Netanyahu’s U.S. visit and party conventions; campus encampments return periodically. (JVP Action letter July 2024: https://www.jvpaction.org/75-groups-call-on-biden-and-congress-to-stop-arming-israel-now/ ; Reuters NYC protest Sept 26, 2025: https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/netanyahu-visit-sparks-new-york-city-protests-2025-09-26/ )
16) Humanitarian 2024 — OCHA/INGO reports warn of acute food insecurity and disease; Rafah assault triggers mass displacement under ICJ scrutiny; aid flows remain heavily constrained. (ICJ order re Rafah: https://www.icj-cij.org/node/204091 ; UNSC 2735 context: https://press.un.org/en/2024/sc15723.doc.htm )
17) Jan–July 2025 — Despite intermittent truce, Gaza’s infrastructure collapses; aid access remains insufficient; ceasefire diplomacy rotates among Cairo/Doha/Doha‑Cairo tracks without durable resolution. (Reuters tracker Feb–Mar 2025: links in #11 ; U.S.–Egypt–Qatar joint statement Aug 8, 2024 reinforcing framework: https://eg.usembassy.gov/joint-statement-leaders-united-states-egypt-qatar/ )
18) Aug 22, 2025 — IPC formally declares famine occurring in Gaza Governorate, with >500,000 people in IPC Phase 5 and expansion projected southward; UN agencies and WHO warn of accelerating deaths absent unfettered access. (WHO news: https://www.who.int/news/item/22-08-2025-famine-confirmed-for-first-time-in-gaza ; EU/IPC snapshot PDF: https://civil-protection-humanitarian-aid.ec.europa.eu/system/files/2025-08/IPC_Gaza_Strip_Acute_Food_Insecurity_Malnutrition_July_Sept2025_Special_Snapshot.pdf ; ReliefWeb: https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/gaza-strip-famine-confirmed-gaza-governorate-projected-expand-1-july-30-september-2025-published-22-august-2025 )
19) Late Aug–Sep 2025 — Aid constraints worsen: closure of the Zikim corridor sharply reduces deliveries to northern Gaza; community kitchens shutter; UN warns of child malnutrition surge. (Reuters Sept 26, 2025: https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/aid-route-closure-worsens-shortages-famine-struck-northern-gaza-2025-09-26/ )
20) Sept 2025 — Wave of recognitions: Britain, Canada, France and Australia (among others) recognize Palestinian statehood around the UN General Assembly, over U.S. and Israeli objections. (Reuters Sept 21–22, 2025: https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/britain-australia-canada-recognise-palestinian-state-2025-09-21/ ; https://www.reuters.com/world/why-is-france-recognising-palestinian-statehood-who-else-has-2024-05-23/ ; reactions roundup: https://www.reuters.com/world/uk/reaction-recognition-palestinian-state-by-uk-canada-australia-2025-09-21/ )
21) Sept 2025 — ICC warrants increasingly complicate Israeli diplomacy and travel; reports of route changes to avoid ICC‑member airspace; some states restrict entry by Israeli officials. (AP Slovenia ban Sept 25, 2025: https://apnews.com/article/32f77e2f2657fbfd82af7a2b632f2cc0 ; Guardian on flight routing risk: https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/sep/25/netanyahu-flies-circuitous-route-to-un-summit-amid-reporarrest-fears )
22) U.S. domestic politics 2025 — Congress continues ICC‑sanctions drive; progressive Democrats push arms conditions/embargo; Republican leadership backs maximal Israeli aims; protests and polling keep Gaza central in U.S. debate. (H.R. 8282 history: https://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/house-bill/8282 ; Al Jazeera Jan 9, 2025 vote: https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/1/9/us-house-votes-to-advance-bill-to-sanction-icc-over-israel-arrest-warrants ; Gallup poll: https://news.gallup.com/poll/692948/u.s.-back-israel-military-action-gaza-new-low.aspx )
23) Late Sept 2025 — White House unveils/markets a 20‑ or 21‑point Gaza plan: immediate hostage return within 72 hours, phased prisoner releases, technocratic interim governance under international oversight; Israel signals support, Hamas deliberates. (Reuters/Politico/AP round‑ups Sept 29–30, 2025: https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/us-peace-plan-gaza-proposes-end-war-return-hostages-2025-09-29/ ; https://www.politico.com/news/2025/09/29/trump-touts-peace-plan-for-israel-hamas-has-not-agreed-00584857 ; https://apnews.com/article/4ccf8c6fc6c626b4e3e25f674d628235 )
24) Through the period — U.S. ideological splits harden: progressives prioritize ceasefire/embargo and accountability (ICC/ICJ); centrists emphasize hostages/humanitarian access/ceasefire realism; conservatives stress Israel’s security, distrust of ICC/UN, and campus disorder—driving distinct media frames and policy pressures.
